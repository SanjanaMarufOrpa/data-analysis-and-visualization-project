This project analyzes employee attrition using the IBM HR Analytics dataset to identify key factors influencing employee turnover and provide insights for improving retention strategies.

The dataset consists of 1,470 employee records with variables related to demographics, job roles, salary, work-life balance, and job satisfaction.

The main goal of this project was to understand the drivers of employee attrition and identify patterns that organizations can use to reduce turnover and improve workforce stability.

The analysis was performed using Tableau, where the data was cleaned, explored, and visualized through interactive dashboards.

Several factors influencing attrition were examined, including department, gender, job satisfaction, income, years since last promotion, marital status, and job level.

The analysis showed that attrition is highest in the Sales and Research & Development departments, while Human Resources has the lowest attrition. Employees with lower salaries, younger age groups, and single marital status were more likely to leave.

Job satisfaction alone was not a strong predictor of retention, as employees across different satisfaction levels still showed notable attrition. Additionally, employees with shorter tenure and those recently promoted also had higher turnover rates.

The dashboard highlights patterns such as higher attrition among entry-level employees, lower attrition among senior roles, and the importance of career growth opportunities in retaining employees.

Overall, the project demonstrates how data visualization can be used to uncover meaningful patterns in employee data and support data-driven decision making.

These insights can help organizations design better retention strategies, improve employee engagement, and reduce turnover.
